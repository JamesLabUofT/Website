João Pedro (JP) Fontenelle

OG-CO Postdoctoral Fellow in Genome Data Science

ResearchGate Google Scholar GitHub LinkedIn Email

Biography

JP has a BSc in biological sciences and a MSc in zoology from the Universidade de São Paulo (USP), Brazil, and a PhD in Environmental Sciences from the University of Toronto, Canada. JP´s overall research focus on the ecological and evolutionary mechanisms that are involved in diversification, distribution patterns, and adaptation. JP is interested in using empirical and simulated genomic datasets to model, describe and investigate changes in diversity at population and species level, also focussing in phylogeography and biogeography. As part of the James lab, JP uses molecular, bioinformatic, and simulation tools to examine the spatial and temporal eco-evolutionary consequences of cyclic irruptive population dynamics at genetic diversity and structure, and how data can inform on demographic changes in populations. When not in the lab, JP spends his time between hanging out with his son, enjoying a nice espresso and/or beer, and zipping around on his bike (when not feeding his fluffy cat).

Currently, JP is a Visiting Scholar at Museu Paraense Emilio Goeldi (Brazil)

JP’s research is funded by a CANSSI / Ontario Genomics Postdoctoral Fellowship in Genome Data Science

Role in the lab

JP is currently investigating the effects of demographic changes related to irruptive cyclic outbreaks on genetic composition, structure and diversity at the genomic level using individual-based computer models and empirical datasets. By combining simulation models and empirical data, JP’s research aims to document how different demographic patterns of cyclic outbreaks affect our ability to identify the temporal context of outbreaks using a combination of genetic inference methods.
